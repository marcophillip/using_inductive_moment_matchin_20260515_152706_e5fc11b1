Distributional reinforcement learning (\distrl) has shown significant promise in improving the stability and performance of value-based methods by modeling the full distribution of returns rather than just the expectation. However, prominent approaches like \cfiftyone or \qrdqn require pre-defining rigid statistics such as categorical supports or fixed quantiles, which can be restrictive in continuous control environments. In this work, we propose Inductive Moment Matching Q-learning (\immq), a particle-based \distrl framework that leverages the recent Inductive Moment Matching (\imm) objective. By outputting unconstrained particles and minimizing the Maximum Mean Discrepancy (\mmd) between the predicted distribution and the distributional Bellman target via inductive bootstrapping, \immq learns a rich, continuous representation of the return distribution. Our empirical evaluation on the \halfcheetah offline dataset from the \dfourrl benchmark demonstrates that \immq achieves stable convergence and robust distribution matching across varying dataset sizes. Furthermore, we show that the inductive regularization penalty on the first moment allows the \mmd loss to focus on higher-order statistics, resulting in a more expressive return distribution without the need for predefined supports.
