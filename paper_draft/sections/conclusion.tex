\section{Conclusion}

In this paper, we introduced Inductive Moment Matching Q-learning (\immq), a new framework for distributional reinforcement learning that utilizes unconstrained particles and inductive bootstrapping. By matching the moments of the return distribution through an \mmd-based objective and an inductive mean penalty, \immq achieves stable and flexible distribution modeling in continuous control environments. Our empirical results on the \halfcheetah task demonstrate that \immq is robust across different dataset sizes and that its inductive regularization significantly enhances the expressiveness of the learned return distribution.

Our study provides a foundation for several future research directions. Integrating \immq into online actor-critic architectures like SAC or DDPG would allow for a direct measurement of its impact on online sample efficiency and final performance. Additionally, exploring uncertainty-aware exploration techniques, such as Thompson sampling over the learned particles, could leverage the rich distributional information provided by \immq to improve agent behavior in stochastic or sparse-reward environments. Finally, scaling \immq to more complex tasks and investigating more efficient \mmd approximations remain promising avenues for future work.
